Buyer Contingencies

What Each One Means for Your Seller — and What You Can and Cannot Do
~30 min readRPA ¶8, ¶3L5 Quiz Questions
📋 Day 8 — The Waiting Game Begins

Disclosures delivered on Day 5. Now you're into the contingency period. David Chen calls you: "The buyers still have all these contingencies. Can we do anything if they just sit there and don't remove them? Can we cancel and find another buyer?"

Not yet. During the contingency period, the buyers are protected — and your job is to understand exactly what that protection means so you can advise David and Sarah correctly.

The 5 Buyer Contingencies — From the Seller's Perspective

The RPA gives buyers up to 5 standard contingencies. Each one is a window during which the buyer can cancel for a specific reason and get their full deposit back. As the seller's agent, you need to understand what each one means for your seller's position.

ContingencyRPA ¶Default DeadlineWhat It Protects the Buyer For
Loan¶8A / ¶3L(1)21 daysInability to secure financing
Appraisal¶8B / ¶3L(2)17 daysProperty appraising below purchase price
Inspection¶8C / ¶3L(3)17 daysUnsatisfactory physical condition
Title¶8F / ¶3L(6)17 daysTitle issues in the preliminary report
Seller Documents¶8E / ¶3L(5)17 daysUnsatisfactory disclosures/seller documents

What Your Seller CANNOT Do While Contingencies Are Active

This is the most important thing to communicate to your seller clients. While buyer contingencies are active:

  • The seller cannot cancel to find a higher offer — they are bound to this buyer
  • The seller cannot demand contingency removal early — unless the deadline has passed
  • The seller cannot threaten the buyer into removing contingencies
  • The seller cannot accept backup offers as primary — they may only accept backup offers in writing
🚨 Never Say This to Your Seller

"Just cancel — we'll find someone better." You cannot do this during the contingency period without liability exposure. The seller's right to cancel comes ONLY through the proper NBP process after a deadline has passed.

What Happens at the 17-Day Mark

The default contingency removal deadline for most buyer contingencies is 17 calendar days after acceptance (¶3L). On Day 18, if the buyer has not removed their contingencies, your seller has the right to send a Notice to Buyer to Perform (NBP). But notice — they cannot cancel without it. The NBP must come first.

📌 The Seller's Position on Day 17

Contingency deadline passed. Buyer still hasn't removed. You have two options: (1) send an NBP and wait 2 days, or (2) keep waiting. You cannot cancel without sending the NBP first. We cover NBP in Lesson 5.

Monitoring the Chens' Transaction

The Okonkwos have until Day 17 (June 19) to remove contingencies. You set calendar reminders for June 17 (Day 15) to check in with the buyer's agent. On June 17, you get word that the inspection is done, the appraisal came in at value, and the loan is conditionally approved. The buyer's agent says contingency removal is coming by Day 17.

On June 19, the Contingency Removal form (CR-B) comes in. All contingencies removed. The Chens are in a much stronger position now.
