The Seller's Clock Starts Now

Acceptance Triggers Your Obligations — Day 1 Changes Everything
~30 min readRPA ¶3N(1)5 Quiz Questions
🏠 Meet Your Sellers

It's Tuesday morning. David and Sarah Chen have owned 5555 Winners Lane, Los Angeles CA 90210 for eleven years. They listed it last week, and yesterday they accepted an offer from Marcus and Priya Okonkwo — $1,250,000, 21-day close, conventional loan.

David just texted you: "We're under contract! What do we need to do now?"

A lot. They need to do a lot — and the clock is already running.

Your Obligations Begin at Acceptance

When your sellers sign an accepted offer, they don\'t just get a buyer — they get a list of contractual obligations that must be fulfilled within specific deadlines. Missing these deadlines can give the buyer the right to cancel. Your job starts the moment that acceptance is countersigned.

⚠️ Key Principle

Under the RPA, "Day 1" begins the calendar day after acceptance. If your sellers accepted on a Tuesday, Wednesday is Day 1 and the 7-day disclosure deadline falls on the following Tuesday.

The Seller's 7-Day Disclosure Window

Under ¶3N(1) of the RPA, the seller must deliver all required disclosures to the buyer within 7 calendar days after acceptance. This is not a suggestion — it\'s a contractual obligation. The primary disclosures your seller must deliver include:

  • Transfer Disclosure Statement (TDS) — required for all 1–4 unit residential properties
  • Seller Property Questionnaire (SPQ) — supplements the TDS with detailed property history
  • Natural Hazard Disclosure (NHD) — ordered from a third-party NHD company
  • Agent Visual Inspection Disclosure (AVID) — your visual inspection of the property
  • Lead-Based Paint Disclosure (LPD) — required for homes built before 1978
  • Fire Hardening Disclosure (FHDS) — required for high fire hazard zones, homes built before 2010
RPA Reference: ¶3N(1) sets the seller\'s 7-day disclosure window. ¶11A covers the full list of required statutory disclosures. The seller who misses this deadline hands the buyer a cancel button.

What Happens If the Seller Misses the Deadline?

Late delivery doesn\'t void the transaction, but it restarts a clock in the buyer\'s favor. Once disclosures are delivered late, the buyer gets a new 5-day right to cancel regardless of whether contingencies have already been removed. This is a significant risk — you could have a fully non-contingent transaction and still lose the deal because of a late TDS.

🚨 Agent Alert

Order the NHD report the day escrow opens — it takes 24–48 hours and you can\'t control the delivery. Have the TDS and SPQ in front of your sellers by Day 3. Do not wait until Day 6.

The Chen Family's First Week

David and Sarah accepted on Monday, June 2. Their disclosure deadline is Monday, June 9 (7 calendar days later). As their agent, you need to have the NHD ordered on June 2, the TDS and SPQ completed and signed by June 5, and everything delivered to the buyer\'s agent by June 8 at the latest — giving yourself a one-day cushion.

You call David and Sarah, walk them through the TDS line by line over Zoom, and get it signed. They disclose a repaired roof leak from 2021 and a HVAC repair from last year. Both get documented. No surprises for the buyer. That's exactly how it should be done.

✓ Best Practice

Disclose everything, even things that have been repaired. Sellers who try to hide known defects expose themselves to lawsuits that cost far more than any price reduction would have. Full disclosure protects everyone.
