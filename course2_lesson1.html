The Clock Starts Now

Understanding Acceptance & Why Day 1 Changes Everything for Your Buyer

~30 min read RPA Paragraph 3 / Section L 5 Quiz Questions

It's a Tuesday morning. Your buyers, Marcus and Priya Okonkwo, have been searching for their first home for six months. They've lost three offers in bidding wars. This morning you called them with the news they've been waiting for.

"We got it. The seller accepted your offer on 5555 Winners Lane, Los Angeles, CA 90210. You're officially in escrow."

There's screaming on the other end of the phone. Priya is crying. Marcus is already texting his mom. It's one of the best moments in this job.

But while your clients are celebrating — you are already counting days.

What Is "Acceptance" and When Does It Happen?

In California, the clock on your deal does not start when your buyers sign the offer. It does not start when the seller signs. It starts the moment acceptance is delivered to you or your buyer.

Acceptance happens when all of the following are true:

RPA Definition — Paragraph 1 & 3C

✦ The seller has signed the offer (or counteroffer) without changes
✦ A copy of the signed agreement has been delivered to the buyer or buyer's agent
✦ The offer has not expired (offers expire 3 calendar days after all buyer signatures at 5:00 PM — Paragraph 3C)

Think of it like a race. The starting gun fires at acceptance. From that moment, every deadline in the RPA is counting down — whether you're watching or not.

🏡 Real-World Tip

When you get word of acceptance, immediately mark Day 0 on your calendar and calculate every major deadline forward. Don't wait until Day 15 to realize Day 17 is in two days. Your clients are counting on you to be the one watching the clock.

The Master Timeline — Every Deadline Marcus & Priya Face

The RPA sets default timelines for every milestone in the transaction. These are not suggestions. They are contractual deadlines. Missing them without a written extension puts your buyer at risk of losing their contingencies — or worse, their deposit.

Here is what the clock looks like from Day 0 for the Okonkwos' purchase of 5555 Winners Lane:

Why "Calendar Days" Matters

Most deadlines in the RPA are counted in calendar days — not business days. That means weekends and holidays count. The only exception is the initial deposit (EMD), which uses business days per Paragraph 3D(1).

📅 Quick Example — The Okonkwos' Deal

Acceptance is delivered on a Monday. Day 1 is Tuesday. Day 17 (the big contingency deadline) falls on a Wednesday — two and a half weeks later. That includes two full weekends. Plan accordingly. If Day 17 falls on a Saturday or Sunday, the deadline still applies — the RPA does not automatically extend it.

This is one of the most common mistakes new agents make. They think a deadline that falls on a weekend gets pushed to Monday. It does not — unless both parties agree in writing to extend it.

Your Job From Day 1

As the buyer's agent, your role is to be the navigator. You are not the inspector, the lender, or the escrow officer. But you are the one who makes sure everyone is doing their job on time. Here's what that looks like in practice:

✅ Day 1 Agent Checklist

✦ Build your deal calendar — mark every deadline from Day 0
✦ Confirm EMD wire is initiated immediately
✦ Contact the lender — confirm loan application is moving
✦ Schedule the home inspection before Day 10
✦ Request all seller disclosures if not already received
✦ Confirm escrow has opened and instructions are out
✦ Check if property is an HOA — if yes, flag the extra timeline

🏡 Pro Move

Text your buyers a simple "Deal Calendar" on Day 1. List every deadline with the date. When clients can see the timeline, they take it seriously — and they trust you more. It also protects you if anything goes sideways later.

⚖️ Stay Within Your License — Important Reminder

Your job is to track deadlines, communicate them clearly, and advise your buyer on their options. You are NOT an attorney, lender, or escrow officer. If your buyer has questions about legal consequences of missing a deadline or tax implications of the purchase, refer them to the appropriate professional. Staying in your lane protects you and your client.

Lesson Summary

Acceptance is Day 0. The moment that signed offer is delivered, every deadline in the RPA starts counting. Your buyer has 17 days (default) to complete all major contingencies. Close of escrow defaults to 30 days. Calendar days — not business days — govern almost every deadline.

Your job as the buyer's agent is to build the deal calendar on Day 1, keep everyone on track, and never let a deadline slip past without a written extension in place. The clock doesn't care if your buyer forgot — but the contract does.
