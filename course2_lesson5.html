When the Seller Pushes Back

Understanding the Notice to Buyer to Perform (NBP)

📖 THE STORY CONTINUES

It is Day 19. The contingency deadline was Day 17. You submitted a written extension request but the seller's agent never responded and no amendment was signed.

This morning your phone buzzes. Email from the listing agent — with an attachment.

It's a Notice to Buyer to Perform. The clock just started on a 2-day countdown. Your buyers' deal and their deposit depends on what you do in the next 48 hours.

What Is the Notice to Buyer to Perform?

The Notice to Buyer to Perform (C.A.R. Form NBP) is a formal written notice from the seller: "You missed a deadline. You have 2 days to fix it — or I have the right to cancel."

It is the required first step before a seller can legally cancel in California. Without an NBP, a seller cannot unilaterally cancel — no matter how late the buyer is.

RPA Paragraph 14C(1) — When the Seller Can Send an NBP

Seller may send an NBP when the buyer: (1) has not removed a contingency by the deadline; (2) has not met a specific contractual obligation (deposit, verification, loan letter, etc.); or (3) has not performed any other buyer obligation.

What Happens to the Deposit After an NBP?

RPA Paragraph 14C(1) — Deposit After NBP Cancellation

If the seller cancels after properly issuing an NBP and the buyer has not performed, the seller shall authorize the return of the buyer's deposit — less any fees already incurred. The seller does NOT get to keep the deposit when canceling via NBP.

This is fundamentally different from a Demand to Close Escrow (DCE) cancellation — covered in Lesson 8.

How to Respond When Your Buyer Receives an NBP

1
Read it carefully

What item is the seller requiring? Is the NBP valid — properly timed and signed by the seller?

2
Call your buyer immediately

Explain what it means. Walk them through options: perform (remove the contingency) or cancel.

3
To proceed — remove the contingency in writing

Complete C.A.R. Form CR-B and deliver it within the 2-day window. Confirm delivery.

4
To cancel — use C.A.R. Form CC

Buyer can still cancel during the NBP window and recover their deposit.

5
If NBP appears invalid — notify in writing

If delivered too early or missing seller's signature, it may be void. But the seller can send a corrected NBP immediately — do not assume you are safe.

The Best Defense Against an NBP

Never let your buyer get to the point where an NBP is necessary. Track every deadline. If Day 17 is approaching and something is unresolved, get a written extension signed before the deadline — not after. An NBP means something was missed. Prevent it with your deal calendar.

Stay Within Your License

You can advise your buyer on their options and help them respond within the contractual timeline. If your buyer wants to dispute the validity of an NBP or pursue a contested deposit claim, refer them to a California real estate attorney. Do not tell your buyer an NBP is definitely invalid without proper legal guidance.
