The 5 Core Buyer Contingencies

What They Are, What They Protect, and How Long They Last
~30 min readRPA ¶8, ¶3L5 Quiz Questions
📋 Day 3 — The Questions Start Coming

Marcus calls you on Day 3. "So what can we cancel for at this point? What are we protected against?" Great question — and one every buyer's agent needs to answer confidently.

You pull up the RPA and walk him through the five core contingencies. "Think of these as five different exit doors," you explain. "Each one closes at a specific time."

The 5 Core Contingencies

A contingency is a condition that must be satisfied for the sale to proceed. During the contingency period, the buyer can cancel for the stated reason and receive their full deposit back. Once removed, that exit door is closed.

Loan Contingency — The Last Safety Net

The loan contingency has the longest default deadline (21 days) because lenders need time. Even with a pre-approval, something can go wrong — the appraisal, employment verification, debt ratios shifting. The loan contingency protects your buyer until the lender formally approves and funds.

🚨 Never Remove the Loan Contingency Early

Some listing agents will pressure buyers to remove the loan contingency before Day 21 to "show good faith." Advise your clients firmly against this unless they are truly prepared to buy cash if the loan falls through. Removing early without a backup plan puts their deposit at risk.

Appraisal Contingency — Protecting Against Overpaying

In a hot market, buyers often offer above asking price. The appraisal contingency protects them if the property appraises below the purchase price. Without it, a low appraisal means the buyer must make up the difference in cash or lose their deposit.

📌 Appraisal Gap Addendum

In competitive markets, buyers sometimes agree to cover appraisal gaps up to a certain dollar amount. If your buyer signs an appraisal gap addendum, make sure they understand the financial commitment.
