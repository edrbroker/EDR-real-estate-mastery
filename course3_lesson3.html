The 7-Day Deadline

What Happens If Your Seller Misses It — And How to Prevent It
~30 min readRPA ¶3N(1), ¶11A(4)5 Quiz Questions
📅 Day 6 — The Panicked Call

Your phone rings at 8pm. It's another agent in your office. Their seller is under contract and today is Day 6. The TDS isn't signed yet. The NHD company says the report won't be ready until tomorrow morning. And the seller just told them there's a "small issue with the retaining wall they forgot to mention."

This is the situation you never want to be in. Let's make sure it never happens to the Chens — or to you.

Why the Deadline Matters So Much

The 7-day disclosure deadline under ¶3N(1) isn't just a timeline — it's a contractual trigger. When your seller misses it, the buyer gains rights they didn't have before. Understanding exactly what those rights are is essential for managing your clients' expectations and protecting the transaction.

Late Delivery: The Buyer's New Cancellation Window

Under California Civil Code §1102.3, when a seller delivers the TDS after the deadline, the buyer receives a new 5-day right to cancel — measured from the date the late TDS is actually received. This right exists regardless of:

  • Whether the buyer has already removed their contingencies
  • Whether the late disclosure reveals anything new or problematic
  • Whether both parties had informally discussed the content

A buyer can receive a clean, late TDS disclosing absolutely nothing concerning — and still use that 5-day window to cancel the transaction with full deposit return. The late delivery itself is the trigger.

🚨 Critical Point

Once a buyer has removed contingencies, they have very limited cancellation rights. Late disclosure delivery is one of the few remaining avenues. Don't gift it to them by missing the deadline.

Amended Disclosures During Escrow

Even after the initial 7-day window closes, the seller's disclosure obligation continues. Under ¶11A(4), if the seller or their agent becomes aware of:

  • An adverse condition materially affecting the property
  • Any material inaccuracy in previously provided disclosures

...they must promptly provide a subsequent or amended TDS or SPQ in writing. This amended disclosure triggers another 5-day right to cancel for the buyer from the date of delivery.

📌 Exception

An amended disclosure is NOT required for conditions the buyer already knows about, or conditions discovered by the buyer or disclosed in reports the buyer ordered and paid for.

✓ The 5-Day Rule

Aim to deliver disclosures by Day 5, not Day 7. That two-day cushion has saved transactions when sellers need extra time to answer questions or when the NHD company runs behind.
