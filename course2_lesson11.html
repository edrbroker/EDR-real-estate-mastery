5555 Winners Lane Goes Sideways

Everything Goes Wrong — Practice Making the Right Calls

📖 THE STORY CONTINUES

Welcome to the final lesson of Course 2. This is where everything you have learned comes together — and where Marcus and Priya's deal at 5555 Winners Lane stops going smoothly.

In this lesson we run through a complete scenario where multiple things go wrong at once. Your job is to identify the right move at every turn.

This is not a simple deal. This is the kind of deal that separates trained agents from agents who wing it. Let us see what you have got.

The Full Story — 5555 Winners Lane Goes Sideways

Day 0 — Acceptance

Marcus and Priya Okonkwo go into escrow on 5555 Winners Lane at $1,250,000. EMD: $25,000. Close of escrow: Day 30. The property is in an HOA with a $450/month fee. All 5 core contingencies are active. Default timelines apply.

Day 7 — Seller Misses Disclosure Deadline

The seller was supposed to deliver the TDS, SPQ, and NHD by Day 7. Nothing arrives. You call the listing agent twice. No response.

What you do: You prepare a Notice to Seller to Perform (C.A.R. Form NSP), get it signed by Marcus and Priya, and deliver it to the seller's agent. This gives the seller 2 days to deliver the required disclosures. You document everything.

Day 9 — HOA Documents Arrive, Disclosures Still Missing

The HOA documents arrive. The seller's disclosures still have not come. The NSP 2-day window expires at end of Day 9.

What you do: Your buyers now have the right to cancel and recover their deposit. However, Marcus and Priya decide to give the seller one more day. You document this decision and note that the cancellation right still exists.

Day 10 — Disclosures Arrive, HOA Red Flags Found

The seller's disclosures arrive 3 days late. The HOA documents reveal: reserve fund at 18%, and board minutes showing a vote to assess all units $9,500 for roof repairs within 6 months.

What you know: The HOA contingency deadline is now Day 14 (5 days after Day 9 delivery). The seller disclosures deadline is Day 17 (5 days after Day 10 delivery equals Day 15, which is before Day 17, so Day 17 applies). You note both extended deadlines.

Day 12 — Inspection Reveals Foundation Issues

The home inspector finds significant foundation cracking and evidence of unpermitted work. Estimated repair cost: $22,000. Marcus is upset. Priya wants to cancel.

Three options while the inspection contingency is active: (1) Cancel using C.A.R. Form CC — recover the deposit. (2) Submit a Request for Repair (C.A.R. Form RR) asking the seller to credit $22,000 or make repairs. (3) Accept the property as-is and remove the inspection contingency. Your buyers choose Option 2.

Day 14 — Seller Counters, HOA Deadline Arrives

The seller counters with a $10,000 credit — not the full $22,000. The HOA contingency deadline is also today.

What you do: On the HOA: you get a written extension of the HOA contingency to Day 20, signed by both buyer and seller. On the repair request: Marcus and Priya accept the $10,000 credit. The inspection contingency is not yet removed.

Day 17 — The Big Deadline Day

Appraisal came in at value. Lender verbally says the loan looks good but no written approval yet. Insurance confirmed at $8,400/year. Seller documents reviewed. Title looks clean.

What you remove: Inspection (done, credit agreed), insurance (policy confirmed), seller documents (reviewed), title (clean). What you keep: Loan (no written approval yet), HOA (extended to Day 20). You deliver a partial CR-B removing four of six contingencies.

Day 18 — Seller Sends an NBP for Loan Contingency

The listing agent sends an NBP requiring removal of the loan contingency. The deadline was Day 17, so the NBP is valid. You have 2 days to respond.

What you do: Immediately call the lender. The lender says written approval will arrive on Day 19. You communicate this to the seller's agent and commit to delivering the CR-B on Day 19.

Day 19 — Loan Approved, HOA Decision Time

Written loan approval arrives. You remove the loan contingency with CR-B. The HOA extended deadline is tomorrow — Day 20. After reviewing everything, Marcus and Priya decide they can live with the HOA situation.

What you do: Remove the HOA contingency on Day 19 using CR-B. All contingencies are now removed. The deal is fully committed. Close of escrow confirmed for Day 30.

Day 28 — Lender Funding Delay

The lender calls. Loan docs will not be ready until Day 32. COE is Day 30. All contingencies are removed.

What you do: Immediately contact the seller's agent, explain the situation, and request a written extension of the COE to Day 33 — signed by both parties. If the seller agrees, the crisis is averted. If they refuse, they can send a DCE on Day 28 giving 3 days — until Day 31 — to close. The lender cannot fund until Day 32. You advise your buyers to consult a real estate attorney if the seller refuses the extension.

Day 30 — The Seller Agrees

The seller agrees to extend COE to Day 33 in exchange for a $500/day occupancy fee if the deal closes after Day 30. Both parties sign the amendment. The lender funds on Day 32. Marcus and Priya close on 5555 Winners Lane. The deal is done.

What This Scenario Taught You

Track seller obligations just as carefully as buyer obligations — NSP is your tool when the seller drops the ball. HOA red flags require careful review before removing the HOA contingency. Remove contingencies selectively — one at a time as each is resolved. An NBP after a missed deadline is valid — respond within 2 days. All contingencies removed plus a late close equals DCE territory and deposit at risk. Written extensions prevent emergencies — always get them signed before deadlines hit.
