HOA Seller Obligations

Documents, Deadlines, and What Happens If You're Late
~30 min readRPA ¶8G, ¶11M, Civil Code §45255 Quiz Questions
🏘️ Day 3 — The HOA Complication

5555 Winners Lane is in a homeowner association. David Chen calls: "The HOA management company says the document package will take 10 business days and costs $350. Is that normal?"

Yes, it is. And you should have ordered it on Day 1. Here's everything you need to know about HOA obligations from the seller's side.

The Seller's HOA Obligations

When a property is in a Common Interest Development (CID) — meaning it has an HOA — the seller has additional mandatory disclosure obligations under California Civil Code §4525. These are separate from the standard TDS/SPQ disclosures and have their own deadlines and buyer cancellation rights.

RPA Reference: ¶8G/¶3L(7) — The Condominium/Planned Development Disclosures contingency gives the buyer the right to review and approve HOA documents. ¶11M covers the HOA disclosure requirements.

What Must Be Delivered: The HOA Document Package

Civil Code §4525 requires the seller to provide the buyer with a comprehensive HOA disclosure package including:

  • Current CC&Rs (Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions)
  • Bylaws and Rules and Regulations
  • Current operating budget and reserve study
  • Most recent financial statements
  • Pending or threatened litigation involving the HOA
  • Any special assessments pending or recently approved
  • Minutes from the last 12 months of HOA board meetings
  • HOA fee amount and payment schedule

The Ordering Timeline

This is where most agents go wrong: HOA documents take time. Management companies typically charge $200–$500 for the package and take 5–10 business days to deliver it. If you wait until Day 5 to order, you may not get the documents until Day 12 — well past the 7-day disclosure deadline.

📌 The Rule

Order HOA documents the same day escrow opens — Day 0 or Day 1. It is the single most time-sensitive item in the disclosure package and the one listing agents most commonly forget until it's almost too late.

ActionWhen to Do It
Order HOA documents from management companyDay 0 — immediately after acceptance
Confirm HOA docs receivedDay 5–6
Deliver HOA docs to buyer's agentBy Day 7 at the latest
Buyer's HOA review deadlineDay 17 (default per ¶3L(7))

The Buyer's HOA Contingency

Under ¶8G/¶3L(7), the buyer has until Day 17 (default) to review the HOA documents and approve or cancel. If they don't like what they see — high pending assessments, litigation, excessive restrictions, poor reserves — they can cancel with full deposit return during this window.

If the seller fails to deliver the HOA documents, the buyer can send an NSP and ultimately cancel. More importantly, if HOA documents are delivered late, the buyer gets a new right to cancel upon delivery, regardless of whether they've already removed other contingencies.

🚨 Common Seller Mistake

Some sellers try to let the HOA management company deal directly with the buyer. This is a mistake — the obligation to deliver the documents is the SELLER'S. If the management company is slow, the seller bears that risk. Order it early and follow up daily.
