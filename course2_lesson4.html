Contingency Removal:
The Point of No Return

What Happens When Your Buyer Signs the CR-B

~30 min RPA Paragraph 14F / C.A.R. Form CR-B 5 Questions
📖 THE STORY CONTINUES

It's Day 17. The inspection came back clean. Appraisal hit value. Lender is green-lighting the loan. Insurance confirmed. Title looks clear.

You draft C.A.R. Form CR-B and walk Marcus and Priya through it line by line.

Marcus looks up: 'What exactly happens when we sign this?' You pause. This is one of the most important questions in the transaction.

What Contingency Removal Actually Means

When your buyer signs C.A.R. Form CR-B, the RPA deems them to have done three things simultaneously:

RPA Paragraph 14F(1) — Legal Effect of Removal

By removing a contingency, the buyer is conclusively deemed to have: (i) completed all investigations and review of reports for that contingency; (ii) elected to proceed with the transaction; and (iii) assumed all liability for any related issues going forward.

Once removed, that protection is gone — permanently. Your buyer cannot bring it back.

Form
C.A.R. Form CR-B — Contingency Removal (Buyer)
Requirements
In writing, signed by buyer, delivered to seller
Verbal Removal
Has NO legal effect under the RPA
Partial Removal
Allowed — remove some, keep others active
Never Remove Early — RPA Paragraph 8I

If a buyer removes a contingency without first receiving all required information from the seller, they are relinquishing contractual rights. The RPA states this is done against agent advice. Document this warning to your buyer in writing every time.

Remove One at a Time

1
Inspection done, repairs negotiated → Remove inspection contingency

Keep loan and appraisal active until written lender approval arrives.

2
Appraisal hits purchase price → Remove appraisal contingency

Never before the written appraisal report is in hand.

3
Written loan approval → Remove loan contingency

Pre-approval is NOT final loan approval. Wait for clear to close in writing.

4
Insurance confirmed at acceptable cost → Remove insurance

Have the policy quote or binder in hand first.

5
Title reviewed, no open issues → Remove title contingency

If there are unresolved title issues, do NOT remove this contingency.

After Removal — The Deposit Is Now at Risk

Once all contingencies are removed, the buyer is committed. If they back out without a valid contractual reason, the seller may retain the earnest money as liquidated damages — up to 3% of the purchase price if Paragraph 29 was initialed. (RPA Paragraph 29)

What to Tell Your Buyers Before They Sign

"When you sign this, you are telling the seller you have done your homework and you are committed to buying this house. If something comes up that you already had the right to investigate — and the contingency is removed — you lose the right to cancel based on that. Let us make sure everything is truly resolved before we sign."

Document that you had this conversation. It protects your buyer and protects you.

Stay Within Your License

You can explain what removal means and advise your buyer strongly before they sign CR-B. You cannot tell them whether they will win a deposit dispute if they cancel after removal. If your buyer considers canceling after removing contingencies, refer them to a California real estate attorney immediately.
