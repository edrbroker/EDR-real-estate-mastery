Full Scenario

Selling 5555 Winners Lane — The Seller's Complete Story
~45 min readFull Transaction Walkthrough5 Quiz Questions
🏠 The Complete Picture

This lesson walks the entire transaction from the day David and Sarah Chen accepted the Okonkwos' offer to the day escrow closed. Every tool from this course — TDS, NBP, NSP, DCE, liquidated damages — appears in its real context.

Read carefully. The final quiz covers this scenario.

Acceptance — June 2

The Chens accept the Okonkwos' offer: $1,250,000, 21-day close of escrow (June 23), conventional loan, 3% down ($37,500 deposit). Liquidated damages (¶29) was initialed by both parties. All standard contingencies apply with default deadlines.

Your Day 0 checklist: Open escrow. Order NHD. Notify HOA management company — document package needed within 5 days. Schedule TDS/SPQ meeting with the Chens for June 4.

Disclosures — June 4 (Day 2)

TDS and SPQ meeting with the Chens. David discloses: a roof leak repaired in 2021 (permit pulled, documented), an HVAC repair in 2023 (receipts available), and a neighbor dispute over a fence that was resolved by a survey two years ago. All three go into the TDS and SPQ. You complete your AVID on June 5.

June 6 (Day 4): NHD arrives. 5555 Winners Lane is in a High Fire Hazard Severity Zone — the Fire Hardening Disclosure (FHDS) is required since the home was built in 1998. You add it to the package.

June 7 (Day 5): Complete disclosure package delivered to buyer\'s agent — TDS, SPQ, NHD, AVID, FHDS. Two days early. The Chens are protected.

HOA Documents — June 8 (Day 6)

HOA document package arrives from management company. Delivered to buyer\'s agent same day. Documents show: $485/month dues, $220,000 in reserves (healthy), no pending litigation, no pending special assessments. Clean package.

The NSP — June 10 (Day 8)

You receive an NSP from the buyer\'s agent claiming the Fire Hardening Disclosure wasn't included. You check your records — it was in the June 7 email. You respond in writing with a timestamped copy and re-deliver the FHDS for good measure. NSP resolved in 2 hours.

Contingency Period — Days 8 through 17

The buyer\'s inspector visits June 11. Inspection report comes back with two items: a minor electrical panel issue ($800 estimated) and the HVAC (which the Chens already disclosed). The buyers submit a repair request via RR. The Chens agree to credit $800 in closing costs. Both parties sign the RRRR. Transaction continues.

June 14 (Day 12): Appraisal comes in at $1,250,000 — exactly purchase price. Loan contingency on track.

June 19 (Day 17): Buyer delivers Contingency Removal (CR-B). All contingencies released — loan, appraisal, inspection, title, seller documents, HOA. The Chens are now in a strong position.

The Better Offer — June 20 (Day 18)

A competing agent contacts you with a backup offer at $1,300,000. David calls excited. You explain clearly: you are under contract, all contingencies are removed, the buyers are proceeding. You may accept the backup offer IN WRITING as a backup — nothing more. The Chens understand and agree to proceed with the Okonkwos.

Closing Delay — June 23 (Day 21 — COE)

Scheduled close of escrow. The buyer\'s lender calls at 2pm: they need one more day for the wire to clear. The Chens are frustrated. You explain: the earliest you can send a DCE is TODAY (June 23 = COE date, DCE can be sent on the COE date or after). You send the DCE signed by the Chens. It gives the Okonkwos 3 days to close — deadline June 26.

Close of Escrow — June 25 (Day 23)

Wire clears. Escrow closes June 25 — one day before the DCE deadline. The Chens net $1,219,000 after commissions, escrow, and prorations. David sends you a text: "Thank you for keeping us out of trouble the whole way through."

That's the job.

✓ What Made This Transaction Work

Early disclosures (Day 5, not Day 7). HOA ordered on Day 0. Written responses to the NSP. Clear communication when the better offer arrived. DCE deployed correctly at the right moment. Every tool used precisely and on time.
